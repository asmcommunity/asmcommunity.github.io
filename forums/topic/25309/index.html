I have the same opinion as f0dder, I'm afraid.. people who poke snakes with sticks over here generally don't live as long as him, so it's hardly paradoxical that he died the way he did - and I refuse to believe that this attack was unprovoked.
Since his death I've heard many times how he was 'the greatest ambassador Australia has ever had"..
If that's the kind of image the rest of the world has of Australians, it's better for everyone (exept his family of course) that he's no longer with us. One particularly irksome fact is that this man insisted he was passionate about conserving wildlife : 1) He made his money from a private zoo (reptile park) which doubled as a farm for the Northern Territory croc leather industry, and 2) His idea of conservation amounted to buying as much pristine land as possible under his own name, ie he was an outright capitalist.
Some of you may think I suffer from the common Australian affliction of 'tall poppy syndrome', but the reality is that I as an Australian was insulted by the stereotype he portrayed, which had little to do with my culture and more to do with the success of Paul Hogan's Crocodile Dundee movies.
What is ironic about all of this is that the Queensland government had been paying this man to support their tourism industry.. let's see them wriggle their way out of this one!
