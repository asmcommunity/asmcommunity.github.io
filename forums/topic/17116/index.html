Developing A New OOP Model
You might want to start by reading the chapter on Object-Oriented Assembly Language Programming in "The Art of Assembly Language" at http://webster.cs.ucr.edu. It covers HLA rather than MASM, but the underlying discussion of the memory structures would be the same. I also wrote a chapter on doing simple OOP stuff in "The MASM Bible" for the Waite Group about 10 years ago. If you can find a copy of that book, you might want to take a look at that chapter (though the AoA chapter is a bit more up-to-date on the technology).
