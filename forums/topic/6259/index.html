Visual Basic APIs -> asm
The msvbvm*0.dll exports are not officially documented, and for good reason, as they are not designed to be used by everybody. You should not attempt to use any of those functions, as their implementation could change at any time. And if you were to use some of those functions, that would mean that you would have to distribute that dll with your exe, and that would be against its licence, asyou may only distribute it with VB applications. Anyway, a lot of the functions are just glorified wrappers for API calls.

A lot of the functionality you will need is in freely available libraries, which you link to your exe. For starters, check out masm32.lib which comes with hutch's masm package. If you require other functionality, just post your question here to the forum, and the members will be quick to help you.
