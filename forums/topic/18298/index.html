Usually, the reader software isn't included as part of the deal. Instead, you supply the e-book in a format that is common enough that people will have a reader for it (e.g., text, PDF, HTML). In some cases, the publisher of a program may grant you permission to distribute software for a reader (e.g., Adobe used to provide a "frame reader" application that you could distribute when passing around FrameMaker documents).
