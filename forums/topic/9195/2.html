My apologies for taking so long to get back to you about Wayne Radburn's method of REPNE SCASD. I finally looked at his code. It appears to be somewhat clunky. For instance, why does he do a JE after the table is searched? Once the index is found in the message table, it is a trival matter to do a indirect jump into the address table, and onto the correct message handler. And by putting the address for DefWindowProc on the end like I do, the default jump is taken automatically. Furthermore, my method does not clobber EBP. That's nice, because Intel CPUs are register challenged/starved. In addition, my code delivers the registers to the message handlers loaded with useful values for processing, and rearranges the stack for a quick EBX,EBP,ESI,EDI restoration and exit back to Windows.
I have read that REPNE SCASD is not as fast as the compare and jump instructions. I have also read the opposite; that double word scans are in fact faster. It is very confusing to know what is true, especially since the hardware and code mix seem to make a big difference. I would think that the internal REPNE SCASD would be inherently faster, but I don't know for sure. Ratch
