Well, there are a few reasons why a company cannot keep that much cash on hand. First is taxes, cash on hand is not really cash on hand but can be considered rotating funds and therefore taxable. Second the 50 billion was originally supposed to pay off the stupid lawsuits against Microsoft and in the end it is not really necessary. Third the money just isn't yeilding enough profit by sitting around, a company must meet certain profit margins for every dollar in order to remain solvent, cash sitting in the bank might yeild 1 or 2 %, that is not enough for even a small company. The problem is that the money does not belong to Microsoft, it belongs to it's shareholders and it has to be spent in order to meet thier obligations as a publicly held company. Now, they have a few choices, in order to meet the obligation of enhancing shareholder value they can invest the cash in new technology and R&D, they can acquire another company or they can distribute the cash through yeilds on the shares. Yeilds on the shares would be the worst idea, it would artificially bloat the share price and the subsequent correction could reduce the actual value of the shares. R&D is the best choice but it is *alot* of money and frankly they would be hard pressed to find that many things to invest in. The only viable outlet for the money is the acquisition of another company, they have already been slapped on the wrist for buying their competition in IT so they must branch out to an unrelated field, my geuss is Walt Disney.

The money will have to be spent or allocated before the end of their fiscal year and shareholders meeting in July.
