Starcraft in assembly?
For a 3d game (if I can remember correctly):
1 Game Designer.
1 Game Architech (Very different from a game designer).
1 Lead Programmer (This will make sure the other programmers are on schedule, and on quality).
1 Tool Programmer. (Very important early on).
2 Engine Programmers
1 Documentation Maker (this one is optional depending on the size of the project).
1 Debugger Programmer. (An Experienced programmer in the art of finding and fixing bugs).
1 Lead Artist
1 or 2 Model makers
1 animator
2 Texturers (that can work also for storyboards)
1 Musician
1 Sound Director (or outsource a competent studio)
1 or more alpha tester that have not work on the project.
1 administrator.
1 Motion Capturer (if there are humanoid in the game, an alternative is to buy the movements or do them by hand).
1 Quality Assurance Personal (Judging by how many bugs I see on todays games, I think many companies doesnt have anough people of this early on).

Of course for a smaller game a person could do more than one of the previous areas, for example the Game Architech can be the Lead Programmer and the documentation maker. Of course for a bigger game (RPG), you will need more artist and sometimes more programmers.
