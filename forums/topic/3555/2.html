My only solution is to break up the macro - that big long line isn't doing anybody any good, IMHO. :) Aim for pleasant syntax and put the work in the macro - not in the person trying to pass parms to your macro! :) Here is an example of syntax:
.CallStack Parm1, Parm2, etc...

.CallStack Parm1, Parm2, etc...

.CallStack Parm1, Parm2, etc...

.CallStack Parm1, Parm2, etc...
