drhowarddrfinedrhoward,
Not easy to answer that one.  Though not speciific to just business, Chaozhou and/or Cantonese may (and I use that term guardedly) suit your needs.  If you are a doctor, then Cantonese definitely.  They are both considered 'specific purpose dialets.  Just about all US Universities give courses to help you in this need.  One thing, though, these are advanced courses and you may be required to learn Mandarin (or basic Chinese) first.  It is a very worthwhile skill to learn but not something easily done so unless you have a real need (beyond a hobby) this might be to big a bite to chew.  Personally, I first learned Vietnamese and then Thai so it was an easier move.  Being a programmer who picks up programming languages for dinner, I discovered I had an ability in this area.  Also learned Spanish, German, French and Italian and some more esoteric ones (such as Latin).  Having an Eidetic memory is a definite plus here but when you get older, whatch out!  Everything gets muddled.  Anyway, have fun.
