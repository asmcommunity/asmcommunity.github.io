Yes it is...
the greatest forum and we must thank those that maintain the site as well as the founders of it. A cell phone is simply a walkie-talkie. Unstead or being pure voice modulation it also has circuitry to produce DTMF (dual tone multi-frequency) audio usually from a single chip. Signetics is a known manufacturer of this chip. The dual tones are what dials a particular phone number. The cell simply transmits this signal to a repeater and the repeater has a phone patch which ties you directly into a land line. The programming is done in a flash memory inside your cell phone. It is used to control things like PLL frequency and roaming as a part of the PLL scan and generation of dual tones and what ever bells and whistles you might have.
Suggested reading material would be the ARRL Handbook and Communications Quarterley magazine has several articles on using cordless telephone IC chips as cell phone circuits.
